## Design Context

### Users
**Primary:** Private equity investors and entertainment-focused funds evaluating a $2M film investment. They're scanning for credibility signals — attached talent, comparable returns, professional presentation. They view this on desktop in a business context.

**Secondary:** Bosnian diaspora (4M globally across Germany, Austria, Sweden, US, Canada, Australia). Emotionally invested in seeing their story told. They'll share this on mobile via WhatsApp and social media. They need to feel cultural authenticity and pride.

### Brand Personality
**Cinematic, intimate, unhurried.** This is a film poster that scrolls — not a startup pitch deck. It should feel like stepping into a darkened theater.

### Aesthetic Direction
Neo-noir cinema meets Bosnian cultural identity. Visual references: Chinatown (1974) sun-bleached Florida gold, The Godfather (1972) candlelit amber shadows. Dark mode is correct — this is a noir film set in Florida's underworld. Islamic/Quran green (#006B3F) as the distinctive cultural accent, used sparingly for CTAs and investment actions only.

Typography: Morrison (elegant cinematic serif) for display, Avenir system stack for body. The Morrison font is the soul of the brand — it should feel like vintage film title cards.

### Design Principles
1. **Cinematic, not corporate** — Every element should feel like it belongs in a film world, not a pitch deck
2. **Confident restraint** — Let the story and talent speak; don't oversell with flashy design
3. **Cultural authenticity** — The green accent and warm amber palette are not decorative; they carry meaning
4. **Investor credibility** — Financial sections must feel precise and trustworthy, not promotional
5. **Unhurried pacing** — Generous whitespace, deliberate section transitions; this page should breathe like a slow tracking shot
