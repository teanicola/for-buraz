## 1. THE OPPORTUNITY

**There are 4 million Bosnians living in diaspora**, making it the second largest diaspora relative to homeland population in the world. They are spread across Germany, Austria, Sweden, the United States, Canada, Australia, and beyond --- a passionate, tightly connected global community with enormous purchasing power and deep cultural pride.

**There has never been a major feature film that told their story.**

Until now.

**FOR BURAZ** *is a Neo-Noir Crime Drama set in the Bosnian immigrant community in Tampa Bay, Florida --- a gripping, emotionally charged story of identity, guilt, belonging, and the dark side of the American Dream. Written and directed by **Sabina Vajraca**, herself a Bosnian War refugee, this is a deeply personal and universally resonant film with a built-in global audience that Hollywood has never served.*

---

## 2. THE STORY

Living in a Tribeca loft, with a high-paid job on Wall Street, and a perfect American girlfriend (MAYA) by his side, KENAN's life couldn't be further away from where he started --- a bad part of a small town in Florida, where he and his family landed two decades before as Bosnian War refugees.

So when he gets a call that his younger brother ARMIN died of an accidental drug overdose while visiting their mother INDIRA in that same neighborhood he ostracized himself from, Kenan's plan is to go there, bury his brother, take care of his mother, and get back to NYC as quickly as possible.

That all changes at the funeral, when MIRSO, Armin's childhood friend turned reformed criminal, tells Kenan it was no accident --- Armin was killed --- and Kenan is forced into the dark underbelly of the Bosnian immigrant community with all its various characters, including BOJAN, a trigger-happy gang leader, and LEA, a femme-fatale-esque girl Armin was seeing at the time of his death, in order to find out what really happened.

### Director's Statement

*"In FOR BURAZ it is my brother Tarik's sudden passing in 2010 that's a jumping point into a part of immigrant experience rarely explored --- that of a child refugee chasing the American Dream. Set in the same Florida Bosnian refugee community my family landed in, this is a story of identity, the deep desire to belong (and yet feeling out of place everywhere), the gray zone in which good and bad reside, and guilt --- themes that have haunted me most of my life, and all of my artistic career.*

*This struggle of a child refugee/immigrant is by no means unique to Bosnians. I believe all immigrant groups grapple with this phenomenon, and it's my goal to use this intimate, personal story to expose the universal truth of this experience."*

**--- Sabina Vajraca, Director/Writer**

## 4. REVENUE PROJECTIONS

The following projections are modeled across three scenarios --- Basic, Good, and Excellent --- based on the actual attached cast, covering 13 international territories and 7 revenue channels (Theatrical, PVOD/EST, SVOD, TVOD, AVOD/FAST, Pay TV, and Physical). All figures represent total worldwide gross revenue.

### Scenario Overview

| | **BASIC** | **GOOD** | **EXCELLENT** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Festival Outcome | Limited release, 75-85% RT | Sundance selection, 88-94% RT | Major prize, 95%+ RT |
| **Total Gross Revenue** | **$0.6M --- $1.8M** | **$2.6M --- $7.9M** | **$8.4M --- $27.8M** |
| Foreign Pre-Sales (Floor) | $104K --- $250K | $104K --- $250K | $104K --- $250K |
| **Est. Producer Net** | **$100K --- $500K** | **$600K --- $2.5M** | **$2.1M --- $9.7M** |
| Production Budget | $1.5M | $1.5M | $1.5M |
| **Est. ROI** | **Break-even risk** | **+40% to +167%** | **+140% to +547%** |

### Revenue Channel Breakdown

Streaming and digital windows account for 55--65% of total revenue across all three scenarios. Theatrical is essential as the catalyst --- it drives press, awards eligibility, SVOD license value, and PVOD pricing power --- but the actual cash flows disproportionately through digital. This is the modern indie economic model.

**Diaspora territories (Germany/Austria, Scandinavia, Bosnia/Ex-Yugoslavia) represent an outsized share of revenue** relative to market size --- accounting for approximately 20--30% of total international revenue depending on scenario. This is the film's unique structural advantage: a built-in, passionate audience that no comparable indie drama can claim.

### Key Revenue Drivers

- Emile Hirsch provides the US name recognition floor necessary for domestic distribution deals and PVOD/SVOD licensing. His filmography (Into the Wild, Once Upon a Time in Hollywood) signals quality to platform buyers.
- Balkan cast (Prica, Salihbegovic) are a genuine asset for diaspora marketing --- the community will rally around "their" actors in a way that transcends traditional P&A spending.
- Julian Maroun's AACTA nomination and credits on Mr Inbetween (FX) and Romper Stomper (STAN) make him a marketable name in Australia/NZ, adding $120K--$340K in that territory alone under the Good scenario.
- Cast cost savings ($110K--$230K total) allow substantially more of the budget to go toward production value --- which is the single most important factor in festival selection, which in turn drives every subsequent revenue dollar.

## 5. RECOMMENDED FINANCING PLAN

Based on the revenue projections, diaspora market dynamics, and the current cast package, we recommend a production budget of $1.5M--$2M financed through the following structure:

| **FINANCING SOURCE** | **AMOUNT / DETAILS** |
|---|---|
| Foreign Pre-Sales | $104K --- $250K guaranteed floor (Germany/Austria, Scandinavia, Bosnia, AU/NZ). Hirsch provides minimum sales agent floor; Balkan cast adds pre-sale value in diaspora territories. |
| Equity Investment | $800K --- $1.2M from private investors. Target: Bosnian diaspora business community, impact investors, and entertainment-focused funds. Producer Tea Nicola's network in the Canadian/Bosnian business community is a strategic asset here. |
| Film Fund Grants & Tax Incentives | $200K --- $400K. Florida film incentive (transferable tax credits), potential co-production structures with Bosnia/Croatia/Australia for additional territorial incentives. Sabina's existing relationships with Stowe Story Labs, LANDO Grant (finalist), and other grant bodies provide a pipeline. |
| Gap/Mezzanine Financing | $100K --- $200K if needed, secured against unsold foreign territories and domestic distribution value. |
| **TOTAL TARGET** | **$1.5M --- $2.0M** |

### Investor De-Risk Strategy

1. Foreign pre-sales provide a guaranteed revenue floor of $104K--$250K before a single frame is shot, de-risking 7--17% of the budget immediately.
2. The 4-million-strong Bosnian diaspora represents a built-in theatrical and streaming audience that is not dependent on critical reception or festival selection --- this community will show up regardless.
3. Emile Hirsch's attachment ensures domestic distribution viability and provides SVOD/PVOD licensing value even in the Basic scenario.
4. The producing team's track record (Paul Miller: Cannes, Berlin, Venice, Sundance premieres; Diane Houslin: Apple+, Disney/Hulu, 2026 BAFTA nominee; Tea Nicola: successful exit as CEO of WealthBar) provides both creative and business credibility.
5. The film's low cast cost ($110K--$230K) means the overwhelming majority of the budget goes to screen --- production value that maximizes festival selection chances, which is the single biggest driver of revenue in the Good and Excellent scenarios.

## 7. WHY THIS FILM. WHY NOW.

The immigrant experience is the defining story of our time. From Sundance breakouts to Oscar winners, audiences and the industry have demonstrated an insatiable appetite for authentic, culturally specific stories told by filmmakers from within those communities. Films like Anora, Minari, The Farewell, and A Separation have proven that intimate stories rooted in specific immigrant experiences can achieve both critical acclaim and commercial success.

FOR BURAZ occupies a unique space in this landscape. It is not simply an "immigrant story" --- it is a crime drama, a mystery, a family tragedy, and a character study, all wrapped in a world audiences have never seen on screen. The Bosnian-American community in Florida is vivid, colorful, heartbreaking, and funny in ways that feel genuinely new.

The script has already earned significant recognition: FEST Pitching Forum Winner (2021), Tangerine Fellowship at Stowe Story Labs (2019), Top 100 Capital Funds Screenplay Competition (2022), Austin Film Festival Second Rounder (2018), Nantucket Screenwriters Colony Official Selection (2019), Lynn Shelton's Of a Certain Age Grant Finalist (2021), and LANDO Grant Finalist (2024).

With Emile Hirsch attached, a world-class producing team, and the backing of a 4-million-strong global diaspora, **FOR BURAZ** is positioned to be one of the most compelling independent films of its release year --- and a landmark moment for a community that has been waiting decades to see its story told on screen.
